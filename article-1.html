Lucresse, a RAL Institute/ESSF raw-talent trainee schooling at Adjarra

by Abèbi

Some people are born with attitude, but others are born with good physical attributes. Some people are born with intelligence, but others are born with talent and grace.
Lucresse is born with talent and grace.

This Saturday 23 October 2021, as the morning lazily drags on with its sweet eyes blinking at the imminent noon that visibly delights in being desired, Lucresse broke onto
the scene of the certificate giving ceremony at Ecole Supérieure Sainte Félicité, in a playback musical performance to fill attending parents, relatives, classmates, and guests
with the envy-lifting melody and symphony of her voice.

Lucresse Dohoungbé, an amazing young singer in her early twenties, full of energy and life, is one of the most talented students in the Department of English at the University
center of Adjarra and a RAL Institute/ESSF trainee. She started the 200 level at the Department of English after a summer.

Freed from the shackles of fear and anxiety to speak English and to take the floor to this end in front of any attending audience by the 2021 EPST training, Lucresse literally woke
up to and dazzled the RAL Institute English training certificate giving ceremony with her talents.

As soon as she appeared on the stage, the audience could see that this blossoming lady oozes confidence, charm and grace. Instantly, she grabbed attentions with her charming
voice, mannerisms and facial expressions that send everyone in the audience cracking up.

Lucresse has an astonishing angelic voice that turned the lyrics of Alexandra Burke’s Hallelujah into a mind blowing and heart touching song.

Her dream is to break onto the international scene as a singer, actress and comedian. In the meantime, she is putting muscles on her knowledge at Adjarra campus, sharpening her language skills at RAL Institute/ESSF and working daily to polish her raw talents.
